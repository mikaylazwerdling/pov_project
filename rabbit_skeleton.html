The common rabbit, Oryctolagus cuniculus, is a species of rabbit native to southwestern Europe and northwest Africa. It has become a very widespread species, and is considered an invasive one- especially in Australia, due to the lack of natural predators there. When not feeding (above ground), the common rabbit can be found in underground burrows called warrens. Members of this species are born altricial (blind, hairless, and completely dependent), one major difference between them and the related, precocial hares.
